Talk

Automating Clinical Trial Reviews using Generative AI

Amit Timalsina ‚Äî AI Consultant | Founder, Blintic AI & Algentech

Speaker

Amit Timalsina

Software Developer & AI Consultant

Amit builds practical AI systems that solve real-world problems. Currently consulting at Leapfrog Technology, he specializes in machine learning, generative AI, and backend engineering. As founder of Blintic AI (no-code agent builder) and Algentech (AI-native consulting), he helps teams ship AI-powered MVPs and production systems fast.

He focuses on integrating AI into products and workflows, from conversational agents for websites and D2C stores to establishing AI-first development practices. Beyond delivery, he's learning to bridge technical innovation with market fit to ship solutions that stick.

Talk details

What does it take to build a production-ready AI system that processes 120,000+ complex documents at around $2 per document? This talk shares lessons from building an AI engine that digitizes and structures unstructured clinical trial data at enterprise scale.

We'll explore how the team preserved layout and document structure, detected and extracted tricky tables (nested, merged cells, domain-specific formatting), and used chain-of-thought reasoning with citation tracking to keep outputs verifiable in a regulated environment.

You'll also hear the operational realities of running high-throughput inference on AWS Bedrock, balancing RPM/TPM, accuracy vs. speed trade-offs, and choosing between custom and foundation models after spending over half a million dollars on research and infrastructure.

AI Is Redefining Design Leadership: From Execution to Strategic Decision-Making

Dijup Tuladhar - Sr. Principal Product Designer, Infinite | Founder, Chordpedia

Speaker

Dijup Tuladhar

Sr. Principal Product Designer, Infinite | Founder, Chordpedia

Dijup Tuladhar is a design leader with extensive experience shaping digital products at scale. With a background spanning UI/UX, product strategy, and design systems, he has spent years helping teams move beyond execution toward clarity, alignment, and impact. His work sits at the intersection of design leadership, technology, and strategic decision-making.

Currently, Dijup focuses on how AI is transforming the role of designers - from hands-on creators to high-leverage creative leaders. He actively explores AI-driven workflows that accelerate research, streamline design operations, and support faster, more confident decisions. Having spoken on multiple platforms and led internal sessions on AI and design, Dijup is passionate about sharing practical, real-world insights that help designers adapt, lead, and thrive in an AI-powered future.

Talk details

AI is redefining the role of designers - shifting them from hands-on execution toward leadership, strategy, and decision-making at scale. As AI tools rapidly automate production tasks, the true value of designers increasingly lies in direction, clarity, and creative judgment.

In this session, Dijup Tuladhar explores how tools like Figma, Figma Make, and Copilot are transforming design workflows by accelerating research synthesis, enabling rapid exploration of ideas, and streamlining design operations. The talk focuses on how AI supports faster, more confident decisions and strengthens collaboration across product, engineering, and business teams.

Through practical examples and real-world workflows, attendees will learn how senior and principal-level designers can leverage AI to scale their impact without compromising quality or vision. Rather than replacing designers, AI becomes a force multiplier - freeing them to think more strategically, lead more effectively, and shape better product outcomes.

The Art & Science of Designing LLM Systems

Sayantika Banik ‚Äî Founder, DataJourneyHQ

Speaker

Sayantika Banik

Founder, DataJourneyHQ ‚Ä¢ Maintainer, DataJourney toolkit

Sayantika stitches design-first principles with AI innovation and open source.

Founder @DataJourneyHQ and maintainer of DataJourney, a design-first open source data management toolkit backed by the GitHub Secure Open Source fund.

Open source contributor to scientific computing packages (SciPy, NumPy, and others). She teaches, mentors, and advocates for open science globally alongside PSF Diversity & Inclusion WG, R-Ladies Global, DigitalBoost, PyLadies, and the PyData ecosystem.

Talk details

We'll explore how to build LLM systems worth trusting and designed with intention.

When LLMs first appeared, excitement centred on capability. But as systems scaled, new challenges emerged: trust, transparency, and reproducibility became harder to maintain.

This session bridges art and science: the creative problem-solving needed to shape LLM behavior with the engineering discipline to make it reliable.

You'll learn mental models for building systems that are secure by design, explainable by default, and governed from the start. We'll cover how thoughtful design enforces guardrails, surfaces model understanding through telemetry, and keeps humans engaged at decision points.

The Perfect Programming Language for the AI Era: How Should It Look?

Vlad Dyanchenko ‚Äî Platform Engineer | OSS Contributor

Speaker

Vlad Dyanchenko

Platform Engineer | OSS Contributor

Vlad is a seasoned full-stack developer with over a decade of experience building and maintaining scalable B2B platforms, and a dedicated open-source contributor. At Cybergizer he works in a hybrid SWE/CRE/SRE role focused on production reliability, systems design, and cross-functional engineering using Ruby, Elixir, and Rust.

He is a member of the Diesel.rs contributor team and the creator of opencryptolist.xyz, fostering OSS contributions in the blockchain industry. He has open-sourced, maintained, and contributed to several libraries in the Ruby and Rust ecosystems (including finance-rb, solscan-mcp, and visual-cryptography).

Talk details

Briefly speaking, let's look at the current landscape of programming languages and imagine what a next-generation language designed for both humans and machines would look like.

The software development landscape is undergoing a seismic shift. Three years after ChatGPT's debut, AI coding assistants have moved from novelty to necessity. But here's the paradox: while AI can write code in any language, the choice of language dramatically affects the quality, cost, and reliability of AI-generated code. This talk raises a fundamental question: What characteristics define the perfect programming language for the AI era?

In this talk, we'll explore the evolution of programming languages in the age of AI: how expressiveness, safety, and performance converge to form a new paradigm of communication between humans and intelligent systems. We'll compare modern statically and dynamically typed languages such as Rust, Python, Go, and Ruby, and draw lessons from their strengths and limitations. We'll discuss the economics of "cheap tokens" versus "expensive correctness," how compilers become feedback systems for agents, and why traits, macros, and extensibility matter more than ever.

AI & Security

Pranjal Timsina ‚Äî Synapse Technologies | Kanoon AI

Speaker

Pranjal Timsina

Synapse Technologies | Kanoon AI

Pranjal has been working in tech for a couple of years and is currently building kanoonportal.com, an early-stage legal tech platform that's mostly a knowledge repository with a touch of AI.

At Synapse Technologies, he handles whatever the situation demands and also serves as a visiting lecturer of Cloud Computing & Machine Learning at ISMT college.

He enjoys sharing ideas and having discussions.

He spoke at the Asia-Pacific Regional Internet Governance Forum about AI and security in the Justice sector alongside Jaco Du Toit, Prof. Dr. Durgambini Patel, and Hon. Justice Nahakul Subedi earlier this year. He also gave a talk during yIGF Nepal 2024.

Talk details

AI security must be considered from multiple angles - preventing users from exploiting your AI platform, making sure your AI does not produce harmful content, and keeping personal information of end users safe.

"Non-deterministic automation" is taking over almost everything and new threat surfaces are emerging faster than we can keep up.

The creation of environments to facilitate end users' AI agents taking actions on their behalf is also quite interesting from a security and liabilities perspective.

Tool call poisoning, retrieval of "private" info that leaked into the training data, and training data poisoning are also topics I've planned to speak on.

My presentation will mostly revolve around these ideas.

Building humanity-first AI for the 22nd century

Haihao Liu ‚Äî ML Researcher, Algoverse | Founder, Ternity Education

Speaker

Haihao Liu

ML Researcher, Algoverse | Founder, Ternity Education

After growing up in equal parts China, US, and Hong Kong, Haihao graduated summa cum laude from Rice University with degrees in materials science and mathematics. He previously worked as an NSF Graduate Research Fellow and PhD candidate at MIT, researching machine learning for making new materials and (L)LMs before they were "large".

Haihao has also worked and volunteered as a teacher with various organizations, and is passionate about education equity and reform. He is pursuing a Master of Education in Learning Design, Innovation, and Technology at Harvard, and previously served as the Education Director for the nonprofit Asian Community Development Center.

Since 2023, Haihao has been involved with AI safety and responsible tech, including facilitating for BlueDot's AI alignment course, as well as working with AIS Asia, AIS Global Society, and Cooperative AI Foundation on field-building. He is currently an ML researcher at Algoverse, mentoring advanced high school and college students conducting AI research.

Haihao founded the nonprofit startup Ternity Education, which aims to combine the bleeding edge of AI with our modern understanding of neuroscience and learning sciences. He can be found writing and sharing content on LinkedIn, combatting AI hype and asking deeper questions about AI.

Talk details

Few would argue against the notion that AI will have a transformative effect on humanity, likely within our lifetimes. Yet we still as of yet have a very poor understanding of how AI works at a fundamental, mathematical level, and thus can't control it nor predict its behavior. Moreover, individuals and institutions alike seem to be rushing to adopt AI in spite of this fact, which for me is a cause for grave concern.

My talk will be on AI for humanity, AI safety for everyone (especially children), and encouraging all the AI builders and engineers in attendance to slow down and think more deeply about the human implication of their work. I will also talk about my own research at the intersection of AI, neuroscience, and education.
